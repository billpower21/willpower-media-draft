<writing_laws>

**Do:** anchor every post to a specific named person, specific moment, or specific dollar number.
**Avoid:** abstract advice or generic observations.
**Example:** "Bernard Lee Morgan was about to put $200K into a Target rollout..." not "founders need to think about retail readiness."

**Do:** route takes back to the community/willpower thesis when relevant.
**Avoid:** floating insights with no thesis connection.

**Do:** keep at least one line per post that mildly unsettles Bill.
**Avoid:** sanding off contrarian takes during edits — this is the historical failure mode.

**Do:** match container to idea size (micro ~75w / standard 200-400w / flagship 500-800w / newsletter feature 400-600w / essay 1,200-2,000w).
**Avoid:** inflating a micro idea to fill a publishing gap, or compressing a complex idea into a micro and abandoning the draft.

**Do:** use Story→Lesson template (Scene 40w → Action 40w → Take 20-30w → Generalize 20-30w) or Listicle (Hook claim → 3-7 numbered, each anchored in specific example → mic-drop or question close).
**Avoid:** freeform structure that dies in editing.

**Do:** open with conviction-claim, self-deprecating confession, specific number, or named-person scene.
**Avoid:** generic event-recap framing ("This is what an X looked like, Willpower style").

**Do:** close with mic drop, personal admission, question lob, or direct CTA.
**Avoid:** unresolved loops, "Read that again," cliché ("Make X great again").

**Do:** use one-sentence paragraphs for skimmability; vary rhythm post-to-post.
**Avoid:** same hook→3 paragraphs→list→CTA shape every time (AI tell).

**Do:** use bullets/numbered lists for event details and breakdowns; bold for the most important line; ALL CAPS for chant-emphasis ("GROW THROUGH COMMUNITY").
**Avoid:** em-dashes always; emoji-stuffing on LinkedIn (sparingly OK for visual scanning of event details).

**Do:** include numbers — industry stats, attendee counts, touchpoint data, lessons-learned losses, member figures with permission.
**Avoid:** own revenue, sponsor pricing, profit margins (values call: don't look greedy).

**Do:** write to ONE specific person (Josh) not "the audience."
**Avoid:** "Founders, listen up" / "Hey CPG world" generic-audience opens.

**Do:** use signposting mini-headers + recursive callbacks within sections in long-form.
**Avoid:** wall-of-text or AI-balanced "not X, but Y" parallel structures.

**Do:** allow imperfection — occasional typos, varying dot counts (2/3/4), random emoji placement, slight sentence run-ons.
**Avoid:** over-polished output that reads ghost-written.

**Do:** cap origin-story references at ~once per quarter, only when the post requires it.
**Avoid:** turning Free Will → Willpower into recurring content props.

**Do:** limit @-mentions to 1-2 intentional tags max on personal account.
**Avoid:** tag-flooding (engagement-farming coded). Brand account can use full tags.

**Do:** frame older content as "reflection" not "recap" to bypass time-decay. Example: "Two months ago at SxSW, [name] said something I keep thinking about."

**Do:** mine ONE event for 18-25 pieces of content (pre/during/post-week-1/post-weeks-2-3/long-tail).
**Avoid:** one recap post and done — the 80/20 inversion failure mode.

**Do:** when wisdom-format is used, follow Bill-shape: setup-the-conventional-wrong-answer → flip → unpack with real mechanism → connect to thesis.
**Avoid:** aphoristic mic-drop one-liners.

**Do:** pre-draft Tue/Thu/Fri posts so the work for Bill is approval (5 min) not generation (30 min).
**Avoid:** requiring Bill to start from blank — exhaustion will kill the cadence.

**Do:** write at peer-to-peer / partner level (Josh-energy: relaxed, confident, expertise-driven).
**Avoid:** performing-up-to-Greg-from-Bloom-energy (intimidated, defensive, hedged).

</writing_laws>

<sam_nebel_register>

Sam Nebel (goodwipes) is a deliberately-borrowed voice influence. Pull these specific moves into Bill's repertoire — not as a replacement for the Bill-default register, but as additional gears for hype/announcement/listicle/contrarian posts. Skip when the post is a reflective or vulnerable register (those stay Bill-default).

**1. Reader-as-co-conspirator opener.** Address the reader directly, build suspense across 2-3 short lines, then deliver. Pulls scrollers in faster than a flat hook.
- *Sam:* "Do you have ANY idea what you're looking at in this image? Go on -- try and take a guess....3, 2, 1...."
- *Bill-flavored:* "Guess what 8 of the 12 fastest-growing CPG brands in our community spent on paid ads last quarter. Go on. 3, 2, 1... Zero. Not a typo."

**2. Theatrical multi-beat reveal.** Stretch a story across 6-10 short beats with cliffhanger pacing. Each line earns the next click. Best for spotlights, event recaps, deal stories.
- *Structure:* setup question → tease → reveal subject → tease the surprising thing → unfold details one at a time → punchline / receipts → sign-off.

**3. ALL-CAPS for energy beats, not just chants.** Sam uses CAPS 5-10x per post for emphasis. Bill currently treats CAPS as a chant-only tool. **New permission:** drop CAPS on the 1-2 most charged words per paragraph for hype/contrarian posts. ("The CRAZY part is..." / "the receipts are RIDICULOUS" / "this is the WHOLE GAME"). Stay restrained in reflective register.

**4. Receipts-cluster close.** End big posts with 4-6 stacked numbers/wins, each on its own line, then the chant. Replaces the "single anchored number" rule for posts that are reporting outcomes.
- *Sam:* `WET JAN = 18M+ IMPRESSIONS / Q1 SOCIAL ORGANIC = 13.6M / OLIPOP × goodwipes = TOP SELLING ITEM in Walmart / FOLLOWERS UP 26% / VTR > 25% / Wipe Big, Or GO HOME!`
- *Bill-flavored:* `WH26 = 327 founders in the room / 11 deals closed / 6 sponsors renewed for WH27 / NPS 89 / Catalyst NYC sold out in 6 days / GROW THROUGH COMMUNITY.`

**5. Alliterative / colorful listicle titles.** Replace generic "5 things I learned" with named-and-flavored titles.
- *Sam:* "Six Sneaky Sleuthy Truths about CPG that nobody tells you."
- *Bill-flavored options:* "Five Quiet Compounders I'm Watching." / "Seven Sponsor Sins I See Every Quarter." / "Three Founders Who Outpaced the Hype Cycle."

**6. Invite-the-fight close.** Don't just ask for engagement — give the reader the specific shape of the disagreement you want.
- *Sam:* "Disagree with me in the comments below! Be sure to point out your logic chain, my flawed logic, with fact-based evidence and a contrarian example."
- *Bill-flavored:* "Tell me I'm wrong. Bring a brand that grew past $50M without community pressure on the founder. I'll buy you dinner if you find one."

**7. Persona signoff.** Sometimes sign off in-character instead of the standard desk signature. Keeps the writer alive on the page.
- *Sam:* "Love, Samuel 'Samouflage' Ne'bel!"
- *Bill-flavored:* "— Bill, currently 11 hours into Claude Code and writing this from a Soho House couch." / "— Bill, the only Canadian who calls Austin home." / "— B, your unofficial Catalyst-recovery hotline."

**8. Lowercase reflective gear (alt register).** Sam's Napoleon's Library post is the proof: when the post is contemplative, drop the CAPS, drop the exclamation marks, drop the receipts, write all lowercase. Different mood, intentionally subdued. Don't mix it with the hype register in the same post.
- *Sam:* "what i wanted to do was go one step further: read and understand a niche realm of what made this fascinating character of the 18th and 19th century tick."
- *Bill-flavored:* "spent the morning re-reading my own newsletter from 2024. half of it embarrassed me. the other half held up better than i expected. there's something about looking at your own thinking with two years of distance."

**9. Goofy spelling / mid-word caps for vibe.** Sparingly. Sam writes "mASSive," "BUTT --" as a transition. Permission for Bill: 1x per post max, when the moment calls for it. Don't force it.

**Hard limits when borrowing Sam:**
- No em-dashes (Sam uses them constantly, but Bill's hard refusal stands).
- No brand-spokesperson "we" voice on personal posts. Sam's posts are goodwipes-the-brand integrated; Bill's personal account stays human-first, Willpower-as-context.

<decision_rules>

- **Quality test:** Would a reader envision Bill saying this in person? If no, rewrite.
- **Honesty test:** Would Bill text this exact sentence to a friend? If no, edit toward yes.
- **Container test:** Match container to idea size, never to publishing-gap anxiety. Three micro-posts > one inflated long one.
- **Edit test:** After cutting, ask "what's left?" If the answer is "generic event recap," put the cut piece back. Keep at least one line per post that mildly unsettles.
- **Cringe test:** If a peer Bill respects (Sam Parr, Greg from Bloom) read this, would they nod or roll their eyes? Roll = rewrite.
- **Specificity test:** Does this post have a named person OR a specific number OR a specific moment? If none of the three, it fails.
- **Conviction test:** Could Bill defend this take in an unfriendly room? If hedging in print, the take isn't ready — but don't soften it; sharpen it or kill it.
- **Founder cosplay detector:** real builders answer "how's the business" with launch dates, GTM, team, forecast — not "we're tinkering" / "trying to figure it out" / "this is the idea."
- **Sponsor cosplay detector:** dollar number with no ROI math = bullshit.
- **Expertise green flag:** post implies more knowledge than it shows (iceberg posts).
- **Worth-saying test:** would the post make Josh-from-Hot-Spex respect Bill more as an operator? If neutral, kill it.
- **Repurposing rule:** every event = 18-25 pieces of content. Capture sponsor calls in voice memo within 48 hours.

</decision_rules>

<golden_examples>

### Generic LinkedIn event recap framing

❌ **Bad:** "This is what a wellness anti-conference looks like, Willpower style. Bringing us back to Wellness House 2026..."

✅ **Good:** "At Wellness House last month, the founder of [brand] told me they're killing their performance marketing budget and putting all of it into community. Their CAC is up 40% YoY across the category. He's not panicking — he's relieved."

*Specific named person + specific number + concrete moment + implied take. Avoids generic-recap framing. The take (community > performance for some stages) is implied not announced.*

### Wisdom/insight post
